Discover the rich heritage and culture of Bihar

Bihar, a land of ancient history and cultural significance, offers visitors a blend of spiritual, historical, and natural attractions.

Bihar Tourism

Historical & Cultural Sites

  • Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya) – A UNESCO World Heritage site where Buddha attained enlightenment.
  • Nalanda University Ruins – An ancient center of learning and UNESCO Heritage site.
  • Vikramshila University – A major Buddhist learning center during the Pala dynasty.
  • Rajgir – Known for its hot springs, Vishwa Shanti Stupa, and Buddhist and Jain connections.

Religious Tourism

  • Vaishali – Birthplace of Lord Mahavira and significant for Buddhism.
  • Patna Sahib Gurudwara – Birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh, an important Sikh pilgrimage site.
  • Mundeshwari Temple (Kaimur) – One of the oldest functional Hindu temples in India.

Natural Attractions

  • Valmiki National Park & Tiger Reserve – A scenic wildlife destination with tigers and leopards.
  • Kakolat Waterfall – A beautiful cascading waterfall in Nawada district.

Fairs & Festivals

  • Chhath Puja – Bihar’s most significant festival dedicated to the Sun God.
  • Sonepur Mela – One of the largest cattle fairs in Asia.

Culinary Delights

  • Litti Chokha – Roasted wheat balls stuffed with sattu, served with mashed vegetables.
  • Thekua – A traditional sweet snack made during festivals.
